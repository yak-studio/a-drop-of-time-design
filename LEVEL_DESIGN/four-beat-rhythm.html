Four-beat zone rhythm

Structural heartbeat of the unbroken ascent. Every zone maps here unless an open decision explicitly waives.

Structural law

The four beats (in order)

  1. Ascent — traversal; low cognitive load, tactile.
  2. Clash — arena combat; high load, adrenaline.
  3. Bleed — memory extraction and/or asymmetric puzzle; high load, adrenaline drops.
  4. Breath — cruelty + tenderness dialogue and/or Mountain Rule witness.

Bottleneck transition

Every major combat feeds into a physical bottleneck — crevice, heavy door, low crawl, ice squeeze. Audio muffles; wind; emerge onto quiet overlook. Must read as terrain, not authored cooldown.

Warmth in the Breath v4.1

≥1 Breath per act must be warm — dark humour, tenderness, or long-couple absurdity. Candidates locked in bible:

  • Act I — “Marriage” cross-plane banter before first blood (non-negotiable). Alt candidate: terrible joke / laugh anyway.
  • Act II — Mother hums daughter’s off-key tune; catches herself; Father does not comment.
  • Act III — old private name, no exposition.

Mountain Rule

Two mandatory witness beats — environment does emotional work; no cutscene cosplay. Music drops; scale and indifference.

  • Act II — Father’s plane: ice bridge over frozen lake; ancient glacier; wind.
  • Act III — Mother’s plane: exposed ridge; whiteout flash; summit mass; her momentum almost falters.

Nickname rule

Pip — exactly three uses: prologue campfire; Ending A; Ending B. Final whisper in A can be last sound of game. Rarity = impact.

Level design laws (checklist)

  1. Four-beat rhythm — no exceptions unless logged in open decisions.
  2. After heavy clash or extraction, parents speak before marching — violence vs love contrast.
  3. Bottleneck after arenas (see above).
  4. Plane puzzles = physical fracture cooperation — ruins, ice, debris; no floaty abstract platforms as default.
  5. Irony lock — daughter’s knowledge never bleeds to parents early.
  6. Nickname rule (above).
  7. Mountain Rule (above).
  8. Coalition was people — encounters never read as faceless army.
  9. Contradiction rule — each lead breaks type once per act, uncommented.
  10. Fracture Swap / Charge — major fights designed around swap pressure; dual-boss is thesis.
  11. Mechanic ladder — no act uses a later act’s verb (unlock ladder).
