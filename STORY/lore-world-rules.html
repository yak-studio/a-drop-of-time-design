Lore & world rules

Physics of the world: planes, the Core, siphoning, Temporals, and summit exit math. v4.4 lock set Migrated from production bible; validate against open decisions.

Canon — review after major story passes.

Source: Consolidated from production bible v4.4 (exit ordering, siphoning, Companion, Uncle as translator). Older standalone HTML bibles may contain superseded lines (e.g. “exactly twelve” wording); this page tracks current locks unless an open decision says otherwise.

Setting anchor

The journey is staged across the Aoraki / Mount Cook ranges: valley forest → moraine walls → death-zone summit. Geography escalates with the climb.

The lore math — why the daughter cost everyone

There are 12 Temporals, each with a plane mirrored against the Core (the original switchboard reality). The system balances on twelve draws on a finite source.

When the Mother and Father (both Temporals) had their daughter, she became a 13th draw and a 13th plane. The system cannot hold 13. As she grew, other Temporals lost power — anti-aging failed, mortality returned. Her plane expanded at others’ expense.

Parents’ plan (pre-death): reach the Convergence, collapse her draw on the shared source, make her mortal, restore the twelve-balance. Cost accepted. They were one day away when the Uncle moved first (see plot — Warden beat).

Planes (playable / story)

  • Mother’s plane — harsher, faster read; her momentum.
  • Father’s plane — colder, heavier; doubt and stasis.
  • Daughter’s plane — the 13th; she exists here after death, contained. Companion is present here.
  • Core plane — Coalition, deputized forces, Uncle’s movement space; connective tissue between planes.

Parents cannot touch each other or casually enter the Core. Deputized forces bleed from the Core into parent planes to oppose the climb.

Plane collapse & ghost memories v4.3

Each Temporal (captain-tier, named, limited count — treat as scarce bosses across the whole game) has a plane. Killing a Temporal collapses their plane. Power concentrates back toward the Core.

Ghost memory extraction: palm to forehead; ~15s translucent slice of that person’s life — mundane, domestic. Parents do not emote; the player carries it. Only on Temporal kills, not deputized rank-and-file.

Deputized Temporals borrow authority from a patron plane. When that Temporal dies and the plane collapses, deputized lose their footing — kneel, scatter — not only losing a commander but the reality they stood in.

Siphoning rule v4.4

The daughter’s plane is the 13th draw. While she is contained there, siphoning is slowed but the structural imbalance remains.

Siphoning stops only when enough planes collapse to restore balance. Both parent planes must collapse (net −2 toward balance) for the math to resolve in the “stable world” sense used in ending A.

If the daughter enters the Core alive, she is the 13th draw in person — full siphoning resumes. Any outcome where she lives with a surviving parent implies a world still slowly dying (hidden-cost variant in ending discussions).

Convergence & summit exit rules v4.4

The summit is a place, not a machine. Boundaries are thinnest there; Temporals can walk out of their plane into the Core. Doing so collapses the plane they left.

TAB at summit gate: for the first time, three planes cycle — Mother, Father, Daughter. She is visible; parents see her again.

Exit rules (summary):

  • Whoever walks out into the Core survives; their plane collapses behind them.
  • After the daughter exits, no one else can — her presence in the Core collapses remaining parent planes (13th as event).
  • Order of exits determines which ending path you are on.

Uncle as translator v4.4: He never fractured into his own plane the way others did; Core access + intact plane behind him lets him stand where all three family planes meet and relay words the parents cannot speak across the gap. Function is communication, not redemption. He may offer his plane to alter exit consequences / math — player choice.

Companion: on the daughter’s plane; delivers cost of choices at the gate; implied prior “13th” who was forgotten — proof this happened before. Stays on her plane if she exits (ending C).

Revival knowledge: Not revealed in Act I Warden scene. Parents learn revival is possible via ghost memory in Act II (see plot & acts).

Temporals vs deputized (combat cast)

Temporals — capstone encounters; plane owners; memory extraction; strict budget across acts (~1 / ~2 / ~3 in Acts I–III combat counts in bible — tune in encounter docs).

Deputized — majority of fights; fear, loyalty, desperation; no ghost memory on kill.

The Coalition was never an army

Moral weight comes from kitchens, birthdays, and collapsed worlds — not from treating mobs as abstract evil. Ghost memories, Warden confession, Mother’s hesitation, Temporal Engineer at the gate are load-bearing for this.

Irony lock

Interludes show the daughter’s acceptance; parents must not learn she chose to let go until the dramatic engine requires it. Player complicity is intentional.
