Lore & world rules

Physics of the world: planes, the Core, siphoning, Temporals, and summit exit math.

v4.4 lock. Migrated from production bible. Validate against open decisions before touching exit ordering, siphoning, or Companion.

Setting

The journey is staged across the Aoraki / Mount Cook ranges: valley forest → moraine walls → death-zone summit. Geography escalates with the climb. The summit is the nearest Convergence point — the family was already there when the fracture happened.

The lore math — why the daughter cost everyone

There are 12 Temporals, each owning a plane mirrored against the Core (the original, unbroken reality). The system balances on twelve draws from a finite source.

When the Mother and Father had their daughter, she became a 13th draw and a 13th plane. The system cannot hold 13. As she grew, other Temporals began losing power — anti-aging failed, mortality returned. Her plane expanded at their expense.

The parents' plan: reach the Convergence at the summit, collapse her draw on the shared source, make her mortal, restore the twelve-balance. Cost accepted. They were one day away when the Uncle acted.

The Uncle's decision

The Temporals collectively agreed the daughter had to die — she was draining them and they were losing power. The Uncle volunteered to be the one to do it because he loved her like his own child and could not bear the thought of a stranger carrying it out. He hated himself for agreeing.

He also knew — or told himself — that the parents would never truly accept losing her. That the plan to make her mortal was a hope they would abandon at the last moment. Whether that was truth or self-justification, he acted on it.

The cruelest irony: the family had come to Mount Cook specifically to try to fix things. They thought they had a solution. The Uncle moved one day before it could work.

Planes

Every Temporal owns a plane — a layer of reality anchored to them. This is the source of their power and the basis for all teleport abilities in gameplay: shifting objects or self across the boundary between planes.

Parents cannot touch each other or freely enter the Core. Deputized forces bleed from the Core into parent planes to oppose the climb.

Plane collapse & ghost memories v4.3

Killing a Temporal collapses their plane. Power concentrates back toward the Core. Temporals are scarce, named, capstone encounters — treat as a strict budget across the whole game (~1 / ~2 / ~3 across Acts I–III).

Ghost memory extraction: palm to forehead on a Temporal kill; ~15 seconds of translucent, mundane life — kitchens, birthdays, small moments. Parents do not emote; the player carries it. Only Temporal kills trigger this, never deputized rank-and-file.

Deputized Temporals borrow authority from a patron plane. When that Temporal dies and their plane collapses, the deputized lose their footing — they kneel, scatter — losing not just a commander but the reality they stood in.

Siphoning rule v4.4

The daughter's plane is the 13th draw. While she is contained there after death, siphoning is slowed but the structural imbalance remains.

Siphoning stops only when enough planes collapse to restore balance. Both parent planes must collapse (net −2 toward balance) for the math to resolve in the stable-world sense used in Ending A.

If the daughter enters the Core alive, she is the 13th draw in person — full siphoning resumes. Any outcome where she lives alongside a surviving parent implies a world still slowly dying (hidden-cost variant; see endings).

Convergence & summit exit rules v4.4

The summit is a place, not a machine. Boundaries are thinnest there; Temporals can walk out of their plane into the Core. Doing so collapses the plane they left.

TAB at summit gate: for the first time, three planes cycle — Mother, Father, Daughter. She is visible; parents see her again.

Exit rules (summary)

  • Whoever walks out into the Core survives; their plane collapses behind them.
  • After the daughter exits, no one else can — her presence in the Core collapses remaining parent planes (13th as event).
  • Order of exits determines which ending path you are on (see endings).

Uncle as translator v4.4

He never fractured into his own plane the way others did. Core access and an intact plane let him stand where all three family planes meet and relay words the parents cannot speak across the gap. His function is communication, not redemption.

He may offer his plane to alter exit consequences — a working idea that would kill him regardless of outcome, injecting doubt into whether any sacrifice will work. See open decisions.

Companion

Present on the daughter's plane. Delivers the cost of choices at the gate. Implied to be a prior "13th" who was forgotten — proof this has happened before. Stays on her plane if she exits (Ending C).

Temporals vs deputized

Temporals — plane owners; capstone encounters; ghost memory on kill; strict budget across acts.

Deputized — majority of fights; fear, loyalty, desperation; no ghost memory on kill.

Moral weight rule

The Coalition was never an army. Moral weight comes from kitchens, birthdays, and collapsed worlds — not from treating mobs as abstract evil. Ghost memories, the Warden confession, Mother's hesitation, and the Companion are load-bearing for this.

Irony lock

Interludes show the daughter's acceptance. Parents must not learn she chose to let go until the dramatic engine requires it. Player complicity is intentional.
