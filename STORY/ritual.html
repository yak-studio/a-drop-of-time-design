The ritual

The Temporal death rite. Structural device for act transitions. Four instances across the game.

Locked structure, open variables. The ritual form is locked. Items submitted in each instance are TBD — see below.

What the ritual is

When a Temporal dies, their people observe a rite: a fire is lit, and one personal item belonging to the deceased is offered to it. The item is delivered using the teleport ability — it materialises out of the air above the flames, as if pulled from another plane. The fire responds unnaturally: colour, height, and behaviour shift in ways that cannot be explained by combustion alone.

The daughter was a Temporal. This is her ritual.

Both Mother and Father each offer one item — two per ritual. As they do, the world splits visually: one half shows Mother's plane, the other Father's. Then Mother's plane fades, and gameplay begins on Father's plane.

Structural role — act transitions

The ritual opens Acts I, II, and III. It is the first thing the player sees after each interlude (or the prologue). The act then begins proper as Mother's plane fades and control passes to Father.

The same locations are used each time — coincidentally mirroring the prologue campfire site at each act's staging ground. This is never commented on.

The three variables that change across instances are:

  • Fire size — larger in Act I, diminishing by Act III
  • Parents' posture — Act I: raw grief; Act II: hardening; Act III: hollowed
  • Items submitted — TBD; should reflect what each parent has lost or is choosing to carry (see below)

The fourth ritual Ending A only

In Ending A, both parents walk out into the Core and choose to leave their daughter contained on her plane. She had accepted this. They almost undid it. Then they didn't.

The fourth ritual happens at the summit after — a second acknowledgement of losing her, this time by choice. It is the only ritual the parents perform together. It is also the only moment since the prologue they can touch.

There is always a fourth ritual. The number four is not accidental — it is how the Temporals count a complete rite.

Prologue echo

The prologue opens with the family at a campfire — same visual, same warmth, same fire. The ritual repurposes that image three times at increasing cost. No character acknowledges the parallel. It does not need to be said.

Items — design notes TBD

Items are not yet decided. When designing them, consider:

  • The prologue plants: mismatched socks, lucky stone. These are load-bearing emotional objects — if used as ritual items they carry enormous weight, but they may be too obvious.
  • Each ritual should show a different facet of what that parent is grieving — not the same object three times.
  • The items materialise from the air using the teleport ability. They should feel like they come from somewhere personal, not found on the ground.
  • Mother and Father offer different items — asymmetric grief, not unified mourning.
