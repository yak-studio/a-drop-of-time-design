Endings

Three unbearable outcomes. Exit order + siphoning rules v4.4. Pair with lore — Convergence.

Presentation (length of stillness, final sting) is production tuning.

Framing: No “good” ending — only which tragedy you can live with. The game does not moralise the menu; the gate and who walks first decide.

Rules recap

  • Walking into the Core from your plane survives you and collapses that plane.
  • After the daughter exits to the Core, the gate seals for parents — her presence collapses remaining parent planes.
  • Order determines A / B / C shape.
  • Uncle may offer his plane — shifts consequences / math, not a redemption arc.

Ending A — Let her rest

Both parents walk out into the Core; both planes collapse. They survive; can touch for first time since prologue.

Daughter stays on her plane — contained. Siphoning resolved; world stable. She had accepted staying; parents almost undid it; they never know she chose. Companion remains with her.

Image: stillness; shimmer of her; lucky stone on summit stone — ambiguity echo of cairn. Nickname “Pip” — final whisper optional sting; rarity rule: see four-beat / nickname rule.

Ending B — The sacrifice

One parent walks through; their plane collapses; they are gone.

Daughter contained; other parent remains on their plane. One plane drop is not enough to rebalance — siphoning continues; world still dying slowly. Surviving parent gained nothing but loss. Cruel because pointless.

Ending C — The absolute orphan

Daughter walks first. Both parent planes collapse; parents become absence. She wakes in Core — alive, alone; world unified; no siphoning.

Uncle variable: kneels / drained / absent — gear in snow. Lucky stone; “That counts.” Companion on empty daughter plane. Long hold on final image — player stops when ready.

Hidden cost variant

Ordering where daughter lives with a surviving parent in Core can imply expiration-date world — she is 13th draw alive among them. Document as discoverable consequence, not main-menu ending label.
