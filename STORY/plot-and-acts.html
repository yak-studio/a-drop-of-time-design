Plot & acts

Story spine from prologue through summit. Detailed encounter markers live in LEVEL_DESIGN act pages.

Canon — dialogue candidates marked where workshop needed.

Thematic north star (reference)

Lessons carried from The Road–style storytelling: relentless forward motion; love as destructive force; sparse dialogue; indifferent environment; the climb’s exhaustion mirrors grief. See Mountain Rule for environmental witness.

Game pillars (loop)

Combat — heavy, grounded, uncomfortable; deputized masses + rare Temporals.
Puzzles — fracture logic across planes.
Traversal — tactile, exhausting ascent.
Seamlessness — one continuous climb; interludes as controlled fades.

Fracture Swap & Fracture Charge design spine

Tab swap is the marriage made mechanical. Fracture Charge fills in combat; at threshold, forced swap — player prepares, cannot cancel. Enemies differ per plane; mastery is setting up handoffs. Mid–late Act II dual-Temporal fight is the “click”: involuntary swap becomes precision tool (combat overview).

Teleattack (Act I, after first Temporal kill): attack chain → teleattack input → swap to other parent mid-combo; separate stamina pools extend pressure. Seed of charge fantasy; disorienting on purpose.

Prologue — orchard run

Warm lowland forest, family at campfire. Daughter traversal: joy, specificity. Plant mismatched socks (“they make her strong”), lucky stone (smooth dark grey), off-key hum, one small petty act (e.g. bird / apple) without commentary — she was a child, not a symbol.

Uncle arrives, weeps, apologises, kills her gently; apple rolls. Hold on body; world fractures; parents tumble into split planes. Title: A Drop of Time.

Prologue coda — graveyard → Act I

Valley cold; hand-built cairn. Lucky stone on cairn — neither parent placed it, or both did in the same moment; ambiguity never resolved.

Mother looks to mountain: “We were supposed to save her up there.” Father leaves something at base of cairn; “Then we climb.”

Act I — The Forest · Who

Parents know: daughter dead; fracture; killer unknown (assume stranger / Temporal). Do not know: Uncle; Warden’s complicity; plan was one day from working.

Intent: teach verbs through rune stones and traversal; dual-plane swap; combat as ugly necessity; bond to marriage before Warden detonates.

Non-negotiable warmth: “Marriage” beat — trivial cross-plane bickering before first blood so Warden naming lands on people, not archetypes.

Divergence: Mother “We fix this.” Father “We measure the cost.” Spatial fork for 2D may need redesign; emotional beat locked.

Warden sequence: not a boss fight — a collision. She names the Uncle. Silence is the detonation. Then one-day-away: she knew the convergence plan, told him; he thought no parent would pay the cost; she surrendered the daughter’s location. “I was right” about the drain + complicit in destroying the cure. Revival not stated here.

Warden choice: let her finish vs stop her — Mother’s vengeance vs Father’s mercy framing, not moral UI.

Self-teleport unlock: her plane collapses either way (kill or relinquish); energy returns to Core — unlocks self-teleport (puzzle-only, taught Act II).

Act closes: temporal stutter, planes slightly out of sync → Interlude I.

Interlude I — daughter · confusion

Walking-sim / light puzzle; she does not know she’s dead; sees ghost of killing; tries to run to parents; space resists. Companion present — quiet, ancient, not explanatory. Ripple: possible fleeting handprint / image on both parent planes tying interlude to live climb.

Act II — Moraine · How

New verbs: item teleport (early); self-teleport (mid, after Warden unlock). Graduation puzzle combines both.

Uncle traces: campfire, cairn, broken mended tool — read as pursuit; truth is compulsion / unraveling.

Revival reveal via Temporal ghost memory — Convergence can bring her back; hope worse than grief. Father: “How many did we kill to get here?” Mother accelerates.

Father’s breaking point: mechanical slowdown / refusal to move; Mother pulls him with mismatched-socks memory.

Asymmetric grief — Mother sees the fearless explorer; Father the quiet, dependent child. Father: “You always wanted her to be braver than she was.” Mother: “And you always wanted to protect her.” Father: “Which of us truly saw her, then?” Question hangs; paths diverge in layout. Mother’s hesitation over unconscious deputized — once, never referenced again.

Mountain rule (Father’s plane): ice bridge, glacier scale, no music. Humming beat locked — daughter’s tune; Father stops; no dialogue.

Fade → Interlude II.

Interlude II — daughter · understanding

She untangles tethers; sees parents as they are now; understands cost of return. Lets go — choice to die for them. Companion states costs factually. Irony lock: parents never see this.

Act III — Death zone · What now

No new systems — mastery. Silence increases toward summit.

Dual-Temporal fight — thesis for Fracture Charge. Engineer at Core Gate — Mother forces hand to stone; Father witnesses; “Keep moving.” Father’s moral paralysis later; Mother coerces. Father’s line: “She would have hated what we’ve become.”

Uncle at Convergence: sitting, no plan; translator for last family words. Summit argument hinge: Father “What if she doesn’t want to come back?” (unknowing truth); Mother “She is seven years old. She doesn’t get to choose.” Workshop dialogue but function locked.
