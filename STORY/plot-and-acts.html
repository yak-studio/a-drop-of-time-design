Plot & acts

Story spine from prologue through summit. Encounter markers live in LEVEL DESIGN act pages.

Canon. Dialogue candidates marked where workshop is needed. See open decisions for unresolved beats.

Thematic north star

Lessons from The Road-style storytelling: relentless forward motion; love as destructive force; sparse dialogue; indifferent environment; the climb's exhaustion mirrors grief. See Mountain Rule for environmental witness.

Game pillars (loop)

  • Combat — heavy, grounded, uncomfortable; deputized masses + rare Temporals.
  • Puzzles — fracture logic across planes.
  • Traversal — tactile, exhausting ascent.
  • Seamlessness — one continuous climb; interludes as controlled fades.

Fracture Swap & Fracture Charge design spine

Tab swap is the marriage made mechanical. Fracture Charge fills in combat; at threshold, forced swap — player prepares, cannot cancel. Enemies differ per plane; mastery is setting up handoffs. Mid–late Act II dual-Temporal fight is the "click": involuntary swap becomes precision tool. See combat overview.

Teleattack (Act I, after first Temporal kill): attack chain → teleattack input → swap to other parent mid-combo; separate stamina pools extend pressure. Seed of charge fantasy; disorienting on purpose.

Prologue — orchard run

Warm lowland forest, family at campfire. Daughter traversal: joy, specificity. Plant: mismatched socks ("they make her strong"), lucky stone (smooth, dark grey), off-key hum, one small petty act (e.g. bird / apple) without commentary — she was a child, not a symbol.

Uncle arrives, weeps, apologises, kills her gently. Apple rolls. Hold on her. World fractures — pieces fall like shards of glass, and behind each shard a plane is briefly visible before the screen fades to black. Title: A Drop of Time.

The parents are not shown in the fracture shot. When Act I opens, they are already separated — each on their own plane — at the first ritual.

Act I — The Forest · Who

Opens with: The ritual (first instance). Gameplay begins on Father's plane.

Parents know: daughter dead; fracture; killer unknown (assume stranger / Temporal). Do not know: Uncle; Warden's complicity; plan was one day from working.

Intent: teach verbs through rune stones and traversal; dual-plane swap; combat as ugly necessity; bond to marriage before the Warden detonates it.

Non-negotiable warmth

"Marriage" beat — trivial cross-plane bickering before first blood so Warden naming lands on people, not archetypes.

Divergence

Mother: "We fix this." Father: "We measure the cost." Spatial fork for 2D may need redesign; emotional beat locked.

Warden sequence locked

Not a boss fight — a collision. She knows the Convergence can bring the daughter back and is trying to stop the parents reaching the summit to do it. She does not share this reason.

Player choice — execute or spare:

  • Execute: she agreed to it and will not fight back. Ghost memory extraction (Temporal kill) — parents see the Uncle in her memory. Plane collapses.
  • Spare: she tells them directly — names the Uncle, reveals the one-day-away plan. Plane collapses regardless (she relinquishes it).

Either path: Uncle is named. Silence is the detonation. Revival is not stated here — parents learn that later.

Self-teleport unlocks from her plane's collapse (puzzle-only verb, taught in Act II).

Act closes: temporal stutter, planes slightly out of sync → Interlude I.

Interlude I — daughter · confusion

Walking-sim / light puzzle. She does not know she is dead. Sees ghost of the killing; tries to run to parents; space resists. Companion present — quiet, ancient, not explanatory. Possible fleeting handprint or image visible on both parent planes, tying the interlude to the live climb.

Act II — Moraine · How

Opens with: The ritual (second instance). Fire larger; posture changed; different items.

New verbs: item teleport (early); self-teleport (mid, after Warden unlock). Graduation puzzle combines both.

Uncle traces

Campfire, cairn, broken-then-mended tool — reads as pursuit; truth is compulsion and unravelling.

Revival reveal locked

Via ghost memory from a Temporal kill: the Convergence can bring her back. Hope worse than grief. Father: "How many did we kill to get here?" Mother accelerates. This is the Act II finale beat before Interlude II.

Father's breaking point

Mechanical slowdown / refusal to move. Mother pulls him with the mismatched-socks memory.

Asymmetric grief

Mother sees the fearless explorer; Father the quiet, dependent child.

Father: "You always wanted her to be braver than she was."
Mother: "And you always wanted to protect her."
Father: "Which of us truly saw her, then?"
Question hangs; paths diverge in layout.

Mother's hesitation over an unconscious deputized — once, never referenced again.

Mountain rule (Father's plane)

Ice bridge, glacier scale, no music. Humming beat locked — daughter's tune; Father stops; no dialogue.

Fade → Interlude II.

Interlude II — daughter · understanding

She untangles tethers; sees parents as they are now; understands the cost of return. Lets go — chooses to die for them. Companion states costs factually. Irony lock: parents never see this.

Act III — Death zone · What now

Opens with: The ritual (third instance). Smallest fire; hollowed posture.

No new systems — mastery. Silence increases toward summit.

Dialogue workshop needed — function locked.
