Combat overview

Tone, core systems, and how the swap mechanic ties to theme.

Tone rule: Heavy, grounded, uncomfortable. No flashy heroism. Hits are decisions, not celebration. If combat reads "fun-first," the story collapses.

Emotional integration locked

Narrative is not layered on after combat — fights create guilt; ghost memories contextualise guilt; player choices weaponise guilt. Every encounter should serve this loop or be cut.

Fracture Charge & forced swap locked

A shared resource that builds in sustained combat. When full, a mandatory swap triggers — mid-animation, mid-risk. The player cannot cancel it. Mastery is reading the meter and setting up handoffs before the swap forces them.

  • Mother: faster, aggressive. Enemies on her plane pressure differently.
  • Father: slower, deliberate; high payoff windows. Enemies on his plane reward patience.

The swap is the marriage made mechanical — two people who cannot be in the same place, forced to rely on each other mid-fight.

Signature fight: Act III dual-Temporal (one per plane) — forced swap becomes the tool for interrupting enemy charge cycles. This is the mechanical thesis of the whole system. See encounter philosophy.

Teleattack locked

Introduced in Act I after the first Temporal kill. Input after an attack chain: swap to the other parent continuing the combo. Camera disorientation is intentional. Bridges the navigation swap to a weaponised use of fracture. Stamina pools are separate per parent, extending the pressure window.

Memory extraction locked

Triggered only on Temporal kills — not deputized rank-and-file. Palm to forehead; ~15 seconds of translucent domestic life. Parents do not emote; the player carries the weight. Feeds the "Coalition was people" moral through-line.
