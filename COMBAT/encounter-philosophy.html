Encounter philosophy

How fights are designed, paced, and made to mean something.

Rule: If two fights feel identical, cut or mutate one. Every encounter earns its place through escalation, emotional function, or mechanical teaching — ideally all three.

Design rules locked

  • Fights create guilt. Ghost memories contextualise it. Player choices weaponise it.
  • Deputized are people — fear, loyalty, desperation. Never abstract opposition.
  • Temporals are scarce. Each kill is an event, not a checkpoint.
  • No encounter should read as power fantasy. Discomfort is the intended register.
