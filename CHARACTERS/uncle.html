Temporal status

He is a Temporal — but he never fractured into his own plane the way others did. He retains Core access and an intact plane, which lets him stand where all three family planes meet. This is why he can serve as translator at the summit — relaying words between parents who cannot speak across the gap.

TBD — confirm whether his intact plane is notable / unusual among Temporals, or just how he specifically is.

Why he did it locked

The Temporals collectively decided the daughter had to die — she was an unintentional 13th draw, and her existence was costing them their power and immortality. The Uncle volunteered to be the one to carry it out.

He loved her like his own child. He could not stand the thought of a stranger doing it. He hated himself for agreeing, and more for volunteering.

He also told himself — or genuinely believed — that the parents would never truly accept losing her. That their plan to make her mortal at the Convergence was a hope they would abandon at the final moment. He acted before they could prove him right or wrong.

The cruelest fact: the family was already at Mount Cook specifically because they thought they had a solution. They were one day away. He moved first.

Whether his doubt about the parents was truth or self-justification is never resolved.

During the climb

His traces appear in Act II — campfire, cairn, a broken-then-mended tool. Reads to the parents as pursuit. The truth is compulsion and unravelling.

TBD — is he physically visible during the climb at any point before the summit, or only as traces?

Summit — Act III

Sitting. No plan. He is the translator for the last family words — the only one who can relay speech between the three planes at the gate.

Summit argument: Father — "What if she doesn't want to come back?" Mother — "She is seven years old. She doesn't get to choose." Uncle relays between them; his own position is TBD.

Sacrifice attempt working idea

Before the exits begin, he may attempt to sacrifice himself — offering his plane to alter the exit math and save the daughter. The attempt kills him regardless of outcome. Purpose: inject uncertainty into whether any sacrifice will work. See open decisions.

Function vs redemption

His role at the summit is communication, not redemption. He does not get a clean arc. Whether the player — or the parents — forgive him is not the game's concern. He is there because he is the only one who can stand in all three planes at once, and because he cannot leave.
