Enemy archetypes

Temporals vs deputized — who owns a plane, who borrows one, and what killing them means.

Moral weight rule: The Coalition was never an army. Ghost memories, behaviour escalations, and the cost of each kill are load-bearing. Do not flatten enemies into abstract opposition.

Temporals plane owners

Boss-tier captains — powerful, named, narratively load-bearing. Hard cap ~10 across the entire game; some never encountered (their absence is part of the mystery). Each owns a plane — this is the source of all teleport ability in the world.

Kill effects

  • Their plane collapses; power concentrates back toward the Core.
  • Ghost memory extraction — palm to forehead; ~15 seconds of translucent domestic life (kitchens, birthdays, ordinary moments). Parents do not emote; the player carries it.
  • Deputized who served under them lose their footing — kneel, scatter — losing not just a commander but the reality they stood in.

Budget across acts

Approximate combat Temporal counts (bible): Act I ~1 (plus Warden if executed); Act II ~2; Act III ~3 in main combat beats. Tune in encounter scripts — keep them scarce.

Deputized rank-and-file

Ordinary people empowered by the Temporal order. Majority of all encounters. Their motivations are fear, loyalty, and desperation — not abstract evil.

Kill effects

  • No ghost memory on death.
  • When their patron Temporal dies and their plane collapses, deputized lose borrowed reality — they kneel, scatter, break. The collapse is not just tactical; it is existential.

Behaviour escalation across acts

  • Act I: panic defence → coordinated fear → moral mix (defender / hider / runner) → holding the line to buy time.
  • Act II: prepared defensive lines; ambush; siege desperation.
  • Act III: last stands; young combatants; ugliness is intentional. The cost of the climb made visible.

Special cases

Warden

Not a traditional boss — an emotional collision. Will not fight. Choice to execute or spare; plane collapses either way for the self-teleport unlock. See plot & acts — Warden sequence and NPCs for full detail.
