Enemy archetypes

Temporals vs deputized — who gets a plane, who gets a ghost memory.

Temporals

Boss-tier captains — powerful, named, narratively load-bearing. Hard cap ~10 across entire game; some never seen (mystery). Each owns a plane (see lore — collapse).

Kill effects: plane collapses; ghost memory extraction (~15s domestic slice); power flows toward Core.

Approximate combat Temporal counts (bible): Act I ~1 (+ Warden if executed as Temporal-equivalent kill); Act II ~2; Act III ~3 in main combat beats — tune in encounter scripts.

Deputized Temporals

Rank-and-file — ordinary people empowered by Temporal order; fear, loyalty, desperation. Majority of encounters.

No ghost memory on death. When their patron Temporal’s plane collapses, they lose borrowed reality — kneel, scatter, break.

Behaviour escalations

  • Act I — panic defence → coordinated fear → moral mix (defender / hider / runner) → hold line buying time.
  • Act II — prepared lines, ambush, siege desperation.
  • Act III — last stands; young combatants; ugliness intentional.

Special cases

Warden — not a traditional boss; emotional collision; choice to execute vs spare; plane collapse either way for unlock (see plot).

Temporal Engineer (Core Gate) — not a combat kill; Mother uses his touch on gate; moral focal point for Father.
